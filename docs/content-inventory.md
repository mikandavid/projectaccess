## Core Positioning

Project Access Austria is the Austrian country team of Project Access, a non-profit organisation working for fair access to top universities. The Austrian team is run by Austrian students and graduates at international top universities and helps talented young people from underrepresented or less privileged backgrounds apply to leading universities abroad.

Important international framing to include:
- Project Access provides free one-to-one mentorship for students applying to top undergraduate and graduate programmes.
- The international model combines mentorship, resources, webinars, and a global applicant/mentor community.
- The design language should feel connected to the redesigned Project Access International website: warm editorial layout, bold typography, rounded cards, generous whitespace, soft cream backgrounds, orange/yellow accents, dark navy/ink text, and human photography.

Suggested primary message:

> Wir helfen talentierten Schüler:innen und Studierenden aus Österreich und Südtirol, sich an internationalen Top-Universitäten zu bewerben. Kostenlos, persönlich und mit Mentor:innen, die den Weg selbst gegangen sind.

## PA Austria Programme

Legacy content to represent:
- Mentorship: a personal contact person for support and feedback during the application.
- Four-day bootcamp: coaching by admissions officers and students from around the world.
- Preparation and follow-up courses: exclusive information and training from orientation to interview.
- Community and network: high-potential students from across Austria learn from one another.

## Bootcamp

Key facts from the legacy bootcamp page:
- Name: Project Access Austria Bootcamp 2026
- Date: 23.7.2026 - 26.7.2026
- Location: Horn, Niederösterreich
- Format: four-day seminar for about 40 motivated students from across Austria.
- Cost: free, including accommodation and meals; travel is usually self-funded, but students can contact the team if travel costs are a barrier.
- Audience: students from Austria or South Tyrol considering applying to international top universities. Bachelor, Master, and PhD applicants are welcome.
- Selection: priority for high-achieving students who face social or financial disadvantage or lack access to information/support networks.
- Topics:
  - choosing what and where to study
  - writing a personal statement or Common App essay
  - interview preparation
  - financing studies and scholarships
  - understanding applications abroad
- The bootcamp is not required to receive a mentor; international Project Access mentoring remains available separately.

## Engagement Routes

### Students

Students can join the PA Austria programme, apply for the bootcamp, and/or use the international Project Access mentoring programme.

### Mentors

Legacy content:
- Austrian students at top universities abroad can become mentors.
- Mentors help disadvantaged young people through the application process.
- International mentor framing: 2-4 hours per month can have meaningful impact.

### Team Members

Legacy content:
- People studying abroad can join the PA Austria team and help organise bootcamps, study fairs, school visits, outreach, product, social media, and tech.

### Schools

Legacy school partnership content:
- PA Austria cooperates with Austrian schools to inform pupils about studying abroad and applications to Oxford, Cambridge, and other international top universities.
- School partnerships are free and adapted to each institution.
- Offers include:
  - personal contact person for the school
  - school visits by top university students
  - webinars for graduating classes
  - free informational materials
  - individual programme design with a PA Austria team member

### Sponsors and Partners

Legacy sponsor content:
- PA Austria seeks partners who share the mission of supporting talent.
- Partners can help:
  - with expertise
  - financially, by sponsoring free support for disadvantaged young people
  - materially, with spaces or donations for the bootcamp

## Impact Numbers

Legacy PA Austria numbers:
- 185 young people in Austria have received mentors from Project Access Austria.
- 120 talents participated in free application bootcamps in the last three years.
- 77-84% of applicants received offers from one of their top three universities.
- 139 active mentors support PA Austria at top universities.

Project Access International framing:
- 5,000+ mentors globally.
- Active across top universities in the UK, US, and EU.
- Free support for students, enabled by donors and partners.
