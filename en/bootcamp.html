About the bootcamp

What to expect.

Four days in small groups: course choice, essays, interviews and funding. You do not need a finished plan. The bootcamp is especially useful if you are still figuring out which universities fit and what a strong application looks like.

Topics

What we work on.

The bootcamp is not a generic information day. You work with concrete feedback on course choice, personal statements, CVs, recommendation letters, standardised tests, interviews and funding.

  • Course choice and a realistic application strategy
  • personal statements, CVs, recommendation letters and standardised tests
  • Mock interviews with a Cambridge admissions officer
  • Workshops with partners such as McKinsey and Schwarzman Scholars
  • Funding, scholarships and living costs abroad
  • Application deadlines, timeline and exchange with other applicants from Austria and South Tyrol

Who can apply

Who the bootcamp is for.

The bootcamp is for students from Austria or South Tyrol who are considering a bachelor, master or PhD programme at an international university. We prioritise applicants with less access to private guidance, international school networks or family experience with studying abroad.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need to know where I want to apply?

No. The bootcamp also helps you with orientation and course choice.

Is there a participation fee?

No. Accommodation and meals are free. We can help with travel costs on request.

Master or PhD?

Yes. Bachelor, master and PhD applicants are welcome.

Do I automatically get a mentor?

Yes. If you take part in the bootcamp, you are guaranteed an international mentor. Where possible, we match you with someone at the university or in the field you are interested in.

Exchange semester?

No. The bootcamp is for full degree programmes abroad, not exchange semesters.

When should I apply?

As early as possible. Places are limited and assigned on a rolling basis.
