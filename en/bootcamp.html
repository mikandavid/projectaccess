About the bootcamp

Programme and structure.

Four days in small groups: course choice, essays, interviews and funding. You don't need a finished plan — the bootcamp is particularly useful when you are still figuring out which universities fit and how an application is structured.

Topics

Focus areas.

  • Choice of studies and a realistic application strategy
  • Personal statement, Common App essay, subject-specific materials
  • Interview preparation with detailed feedback
  • Funding, scholarships and living costs abroad
  • Application deadlines and a realistic timeline
  • Exchange with other applicants from Austria and South Tyrol

Who can apply

Who can take part.

Students from Austria or South Tyrol considering a bachelor, master or PhD programme at an international university. Priority for applicants with less access to private guidance, international school networks or family experience of studying abroad.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need to know where I want to apply?

No. The bootcamp also helps with orientation and choosing a course.

Is there a participation fee?

No. Accommodation and meals are free. Travel costs can be supported on request.

Master or PhD?

Yes. Bachelor, master and PhD applicants are welcome.

Do I automatically get a mentor?

No. Mentoring runs through the international Project Access programme and is separate from the bootcamp.

Exchange semester?

No. The bootcamp is aimed at full degree programmes abroad, not exchange semesters.

When should I apply?

As early as possible. Places are limited and assigned on a rolling basis.
